Mind & Mood

Did you know

Six minutes cuts stress 68%

A University of Sussex study found that reading fiction for just six minutes reduces physiological stress by up to 68% — more effectively than music, a walk, or a cup of tea. Your body actually unwinds. Today's stories are right here.

Try this

Swap your screen for pages tonight

Blue light from phones suppresses melatonin — the hormone that signals sleep. Swapping your phone for a book in the last 20 minutes before bed helps you fall asleep faster, sleep more deeply, and wake up less groggy. One of the cheapest sleep upgrades there is.

Did you know

Fiction grows your empathy

People who read literary fiction regularly score higher on empathy tests than non-readers. Spending hours inside another person's mind — following their fears, wants, and small noticing — builds the same neural pathways that power real-world compassion. Every story you finish is practice at being human.

On This Day

1953

On 2 June 1953, Queen Elizabeth II was crowned at Westminster Abbey — the first coronation ever broadcast on live television, watched by 27 million people in Britain, many of them crowded around a neighbour's borrowed set to witness something utterly new together. A reminder that the moments we share, even through a flickering screen, bind us in ways that last.
