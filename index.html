Mind & Mood

Did you know

Six minutes, stress halved

A University of Sussex study found that reading silently for just six minutes reduced participants' stress levels by 68% — more than listening to music, taking a walk, or having a cup of tea. Today's five-minute story is genuinely the right prescription.

Try this

Stories beat late-night screens

Swap the last 10 minutes of phone-scrolling tonight for a short story instead. Screens push melatonin back; a quiet read eases you toward sleep rather than away from it — and you tend to wake up with whatever you read still softly in your head.

Did you know

Reading rewires empathy fast

Studies in social neuroscience show that reading literary fiction — stories with complex, morally ambiguous characters — measurably improves our ability to read other people's emotions in real life. Even one sitting makes a small but real difference. The effect is specific to fiction; non-fiction doesn't produce it.

On This Day

1896

On June 4, 1896, Henry Ford drove his very first car — a spindly, four-wheeled contraption he called the Quadricycle — through the empty cobbled streets of Detroit in the middle of the night, having spent years tinkering in a backyard shed while working his regular job. No fanfare, no press, just a man and a machine and a city asleep around him. It's a small reminder that most big things begin in private, on someone's own time, out of pure stubbornness.
