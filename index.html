Mind & Mood

Did you know

Six minutes calms you down

Reading fiction for just six minutes slows heart rate and eases muscle tension more effectively than most other relaxation methods — including listening to music or taking a walk — according to researchers at the University of Sussex. That's exactly what today's short story is for.

Try this

Name one good thing

Before you go to sleep tonight, write down one specific good thing that happened today — not 'things are fine,' but something particular. Gratitude research consistently shows this shrinks the gap between how your day actually went and how you feel about it, and it takes under two minutes.

Did you know

Fiction builds real empathy

Regular fiction readers score measurably higher on theory-of-mind tests — the ability to infer what others are thinking and feeling from partial cues. Researchers believe inhabiting characters' inner lives is genuine practice for navigating real ones. Today's long story gives you about fifteen minutes of that.

On This Day

1965

On 3 June 1965, astronaut Ed White became the first American to walk in space — tethered to Gemini 4 at 17,500 miles per hour, drifting above a curved blue Earth for twenty-three extraordinary minutes. He said afterwards it was the saddest moment of his life when he had to come back inside. Some doors, once opened, are impossible to forget — which is a good reason to keep opening them.
